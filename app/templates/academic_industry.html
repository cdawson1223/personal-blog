My "Career" Journey (so far...)

I consider myself a seasoned intern but a rookie in full-time jobs. I'm currently wrapping up my third internship. Now, I don't really want to use this space to just talk about the feats and projects I did as an intern because in my opinion, that's what a LinkedIn or Resume are for. Instead, I'd rather talk about the unique journey I've had and the experiences I have gained from each stage thus far.

As someone who has interned in a wide variety of fields and industires - believe me, finance and tech could not be more different - I'd like to think I've learned someone different from each one. I will never forget the first day as a Sales and Trading Internship at Deutsche Bank. I woke up at the crack of dawn to hop on the Q train to my very first day of work. There had been so many questions wandering through my head the past few weeks - what should I wear? What will the people be like? And the biggest question of all in finance: how long will the hours be...? Needless to say, the biggest things I learned from this internship were: 1. Finance was not for me 2. Maybe software was for me 3. Internship and early jobs are a part of a journey not some ultimate destination

Lessons one and two came together on day on the internships when a Python script to read, reform, and write from a Bloomberg terminal was needed and having some prgoramming experience, it was my task to do it. Needless to say, this was my favorite day of the entire internship - enough said. To elaborate a little bit on the third point - I vividly remember one day at this intenrship in one of those "intern chats with higher up people" that companies love to do: the person speaking said something allong the lines of "You're holding the Willy Wonka ticket in your hand - take advtange of it and work hard! BUT, only take this ticket if it is what you really want." In that moment I realized, he had put into words exactly what I was feeling the entire internship. Yes it was a great opportunity but it was not where I saw the future of my career.

These two events were probably the most distinct memories I had in that whirlwind of a summer. At first, I walked away from it a little disappointed that I had wasted what was said to be "the precious Junior year internship," without any true decision or answers about what I wanted to do. I started to realize, though, that sometimes you have to try things you don't necessaryily like as muh to help you fiure out what you do like. I drew back to my favorite day at the internship: when I was coding. Ultimately, this led me to pursue my Master's degree in Computer Science and begin recruiting for software engineering internships. 2 years, 2 software internships, and (2 classes away from) a Master's in Computer Science later, and I can safely say I am grateful for my finance internship two summers ago.

Now, to reflect on my time as a Software Engineering Intern...

Summer 2023, I worked at SpaceX as a full-stack software engineer building internal web tools. I learned how to build applications using the flask framework, coincidentally what I am using now to buidl this website! This was an exciting time for me because it confirmed what I had hoped: that a career starting in software engineering was for me! Oh and I might add, it didn't hurt that I got to see some rockets launch during my summer there as well! As great as my summer in 2023 was, I concluded I wanted to yet again, try something else - still within software engineering but maybe different. Why? I realized how much you really learn by trying new things and now with my new outlook on internships, I figured one more summer meant one more new opportunity. Software has so many applications in this day and age. Thus, I decided I would target an industry aside from aerospace. This led my to my current summmer internship role as a vehicle platform software engineer at Woven by Toyota. This role gave me a unique opportunity to explore both the tech and automotive worlds a bit more. Thus far, I still stand that software engineering is the career a want to be in - in specifically what industry, that is still to be decided...

Eventually, I want to make a platform for interns of all backgrounds to chat and hopefully provide mentorship. Internships have helped me grow a lot, but I wish I had the knowledge I now have about them going into my first internship. This will be my next personal project coming, erm someday!
