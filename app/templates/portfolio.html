Research Projects:

  • At the University of Michigan I did research at the Center for Healthcare Engineering and Patient Safety (CHEPS). During my time at CHEPS, I had the opportunity to work on three different teams. I used linear programs in CPLEX to build optimal schedules for residents at the university hospital. I was a part of the simulation team that built programs in C++ to simulate different scenarios for prenatal women. Lastly, I worked on a new model for Analytic Morphomics®, which measures bio-markers about a patient’s body in order to assign quantitative values to the patient’s state.
  • At Columbia University I did research in Computational Genomics in the Pe'er Lab. Here I had the opporutnity to look into plasticity within Lung Cancer. Plastciity is an important metric to look at cancerous cells to see how they can change and respond to different treatments. In the project, we utilized a Python tool called scimilarity that given a set of unknown cells, returns the nearests neighbors for help create a potential label. Using these distributions we compute entropy and bhattacharyya distance to create a plasticity score.
  • Both of my research opportunities were momentous in the realization that I wanted to (and could) use my talents in quantitative analysis, programming, and problem solving to help address important issues in the world. Specifically for both of these, the applications in healthcare are endless!

DevFest Hackathon:

  • I competed in the DevFest Hackathon hosted at Columbia University. Here, along with one other partner, we built an application called "Where to Live." Deciding where you want to live with so many options can be overwhelming at times. Our application, allowed users to pick from various featues like cost of living, weather, demographics, walkability, etc. and using various algorithms on the backend helped choose some compatible cities that would be good for them to live in! We used a mix of machine learning algorithms, research for numerical mapping, and putting these all together with a nearest neighbor algorithm to get the best options given the user input.
  • In all, DevFest was a great opportunity to test out a project I had been wanting to try. In 24 hours we were only able to get to a beta version of the app but it was cool to see how much we could build in such a short time. This Hackathon was also a great oppoortunity to work algonisde someone else in a time crunch, meet new people, and learn about the kinds of projects they are passionate about!
